Beyond Basics: Expressing Certainty

Mastering formal and informal phrases for conviction, disbelief, timing, and common idiomatic expressions used in daily and professional life.

1. Certainty, Belief, and Observation

A. To have no doubt / Without doubt

To be very certain or sure about an opinion or fact.

  • I have no doubt about it.
  • Let’s say that you are grumpy staff, but there’s **no doubt** that you’re the best staff of the year. => sem duvida / imaginei
B. To be under the impression

To believe something is true, but it may be inaccurate.

  • To be honest, I was **under the impression** that you couldn't get a parking ticket on private property.
  • Rita must be **under the impression** that it was him who stole her laptop. => suspeitar / ter a impressão que
H. Highly regarded

To consider someone or something very good or important; well-known.

  • So, he is a **highly regarded** staff. => bem respeitado
C. Come as no surprise

Something that was expected; not surprising.

  • It **came as no surprise** when Ford shut down business.
  • The antonym for “ Come as no surprise “ is “ Come as a surprise “ => Isso não me surpreende / Isso não é novidade pra mim

2. Time, Condition, and Logic

C. By the time

Establishes a moment in time when an action will have happened, before another action begins.

  • **By the time** we arrived at the party, the other guests were already there.
  • **By the time** we got home, the baby was already asleep. => No momento que
D. It is only a matter of time

Used to state that something is inevitable, though the exact moment is unknown.

  • People are starting to ask for an explanation. You can say that **it is only a matter of time** until the system fails. => é uma questão de tempo
F. No point

Something that does not make logical sense or is futile.

  • There is **no point** in arriving late. => não faz sentido
E. To stand a chance of

To have a prospect or possibility of success, winning, or survival.

  • If she doesn’t start training, she won’t **stand a chance of** winning the race. => sem chance

3. Emphasis and Strict Conditions

D. By all means / By no means

Used to emphasize something in a negative way (definitely not).

  • The ex-director was **by no means** fit for that job.
  • **By no means** am I going to my uncle’s wedding. => de jeito nenhum / simplesmente que não é

Synonyms: On no account, In no way, Definitely not

I. Under no circumstances

No matter what the situation is, something must definitely not happen.

  • **Under no circumstances** is anyone allowed to leave home. This is a curfew. => de modo algum
E. To be taken aback

To be surprised or shocked and unable to respond immediately.

  • The mainstream news **took me aback** with death toll in China. => foi pego de surpresa

4. Quantity, Reference, and Observation

F. A great deal of something

To talk about a large quantity of something uncountable (time, research, effort).

  • A **great deal of** research has been done already on Coronavirus. => muito

Synonyms: A good deal of something

G. Make no reference

To talk or write about something briefly, or just mention something quickly.

  • She **made no reference** to our agreement.
  • Many of my paintings **make reference** to the modern science. => se referir
A. To keep an eye on

To watch or look after something to prevent harm.

  • I asked my wife to **keep an eye on** food on fire. => Dar uma olhada
B. To have a glance at

To read or see something quickly.

  • You can ask your teacher to **have a quick glance at** your essay. => Dar uma olhada rápido

5. Figurative Language and Idioms

G. Couch potato

A lazy person who spends too much time sitting on the couch, often watching TV.

  • So, this person is a **couch potato**. => preguiçoso
H. Make up my mind

To become decisive after being indecisive.

  • I have **made up my mind**, I am going on the trip with you. => Já estou decidida
I. Let the cat out of the bag

To reveal a secret.

  • I can’t believe Jane **let the cat out of the bag**. => Revelar um secreto
J. Add insult to injury

To worsen a bad situation.

  • The company was living a bad financial climate and with the lost of this contract **adds insult to injury**. => colocar fogo na lareira

Lesson Review

These advanced phrases elevate your language, allowing you to express nuance in belief, time, and condition, which is crucial for high-level communication.
